## Getting Started (5 Minutes)

### Step 1: Generate Your Dashboard (2 minutes)

1. Go to the Mission Control customizer
2. Answer 6 questions about your business
3. Download your custom dashboard
4. Extract the ZIP file anywhere on your computer

**The 6 Questions Explained:**

1. **Who are you?** → Determines recommended modules and tips
2. **What tier?** → Free, Pro, or Team
3. **What do you track?** → Select your modules (clients, finances, goals, etc.)
4. **Biggest challenge?** → Helps prioritize which modules appear first
5. **Business name?** → Personalizes your dashboard
6. **Visual style?** → Dark/light mode + accent color

### Step 2: Customize Your Data (3 minutes)

1. Open `dashboard.html` in your browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox)
2. Open `config.js` in any text editor (TextEdit, Notepad, VS Code)
3. Replace placeholder data with your real information:
   - Your daily priorities
   - Your active projects
   - Your revenue goals
   - Your 90-day objectives
4. Save the file
5. Refresh your browser

**That's it!** Your dashboard now shows YOUR data.

## Module-by-Module Guide

### 🎯 Daily Command Center (Core - All Tiers)

**Purpose:** Your morning focus and daily priorities

**What's Included:**
- 3 MITs (Most Important Tasks) for today
- Daily intention/focus statement
- Energy level tracker (1-10)
- Quick win tracker
- Distraction list (park ideas here)

**How to Use:**
1. Every morning, fill in your 3 priorities
2. Set your energy level (affects how much you take on)
3. Write one sentence about today's theme
4. Check off items as you complete them
5. Add distractions to the list instead of acting on them

**Pro Tips:**
- MITs should be completable today
- If you have 10 priorities, you have 0 priorities
- Energy tracking helps you spot burnout patterns

### 🎯 Goal & OKR Tracker (Core - All Tiers)

**Purpose:** 90-day goal tracking with weekly milestones

**What's Included:**
- Quarterly objectives (3-5 max)
- Key results with metrics
- Weekly progress bars
- Goal revision history
- Celebration tracker

**How to Use:**
1. Set 3-5 goals for the next 90 days
2. Define key results (measurable outcomes)
3. Update progress weekly
4. Celebrate when you hit milestones
5. Revise goals if circumstances change

**Framework:** OKR (Objectives & Key Results)
- Objective: What you want to achieve (qualitative)
- Key Result: How you'll measure success (quantitative)

**Example:**
- Objective: Launch new service offering
- KR1: Complete website by March 15 (100%)
- KR2: Get 5 beta clients by March 30 (60%)
- KR3: $5K revenue from new service by April 30 (40%)

## Daily Workflow

### Morning Routine (5 minutes)

1. **Open Mission Control** (bookmark the file)
2. **Review Daily Focus**
   - What are your 3 MITs today?
   - What's your energy level?
3. **Check upcoming deadlines**
   - Any client deliverables due?
   - Any meetings to prepare for?
4. **Scan other modules**
   - Quick glance at revenue, goals, projects
5. **Close the tab and do the work**

### Throughout the Day

- **Capture ideas** in your Ideas module (don't act immediately)
- **Log time** as you work on different projects

### Evening Shutdown (2 minutes)

1. **Update progress** on your 3 MITs
2. **Check off completed tasks**
3. **Note any carryover** for tomorrow
4. **Log energy level** (how did today feel?)
5. **Close with peace of mind**

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: Dashboard doesn't open
**Solution:**
- Make sure you extracted the ZIP file
- Try opening in a different browser
- Check that dashboard.html exists in the folder

### Problem: Changes to config.js don't appear
**Solution:**
- Save the file (CMD+S or CTRL+S)
- Refresh browser (CMD+R or F5)
- Check for syntax errors in config.js (missing quotes, commas)

### Problem: Module isn't showing
**Solution:**
- Check that you selected it during customization
- Free tier only shows 5 modules—upgrade to Pro for more
- Verify the module name in config.js matches exactly

### Problem: Styling looks wrong
**Solution:**
- Try a different browser
- Clear browser cache
- Check that styles.css is in the same folder as dashboard.html
